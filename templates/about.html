Intellectual property sales
{#http://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/statistics-explained/index.php/Intellectual_property_rights_statistics#} {#<--! START HERE: Flesh out rationale for choosing the seven countries. Complete analysis of eurostat numbers. -->#}

With an explosive growth rate of 5435.6% from 1975-2016, patents, copyrights, and trademarks have outpaced other economic sectors. World Bank data shows "charges for the use of intellectual property, receipts", or IP sales, grew from $5.9B USD in 1975 to $326.6B USD in 2016. Compared with other economic indicators, IP sales presents an interesting bellwether for understanding how countries across the world innovate, develop, design, and engineer new ideas—as well as license and sell branding, design, and creativity in the arts.

    Why choose these seven European countries?

    In that same period, 1975-2016, the European Union (EU) saw an increase from $1.4B USD to $125.5B USD. That's a 8864.3% increase. While other countries around the world show higher figures of IP sales, the European Union has become highly competitive with other global leaders like the United States, China, and India in IP sales. Eurostat states: "Based on the number of trademark applications made in 1996-2015, the leading European countries in terms of EUTM protection are Germany, the United Kingdom, Italy, Spain, France, the Netherlands and Switzerland."

    For "community designs filed," from 2006 to 2016, the same 7 countries cited above hold the highest average in the European Union, with one exception. (For that same period, Poland averaged 2611 community designs filed whereas Switzerland averaged 2227 and the Netherlands averaged 2193.) We chose these seven countries based on two criteria (EUTM and IP /Community designs filed). Germany, the United Kingdom, Italy, Spain, France, the Netherlands and Switzerland were selected because they ranked highest in both criteria combined.
