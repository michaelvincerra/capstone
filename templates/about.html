Why DataPanino?

'Stacked' bar charts look like tall sandwiches. "Panino" in Italian, means "sandwich." DataPanino makes data quickly digestible. Hence, our name. If our charts make you hungry, I recommend patronizing your favorite neighborhood restaurant.

I understand staring at charts can be boring. That's why our a unique model, a "DataDeli", helps you build custom visualizations, allowing you to view data only relevant to your research. Save your views as part of your research or export them as slides. For the seriously hungry, I recommend you save custom views to your profile. DataPanino's target audience is analysts, economists, and investors. But of course, anyone can use it.

Global economic data shows an explosive growth rate of 5435.6% from 1975-2016 for patents, copyrights, and trademarks— collectively—intellectual property (IP). World Bank data shows "charges for the use of intellectual property, receipts", or IP sales, grew from $5.9B USD in 1975 to $326.6B USD in 2016. Compared with other economic indicators, IP sales presents an interesting bellwether for understanding how countries across the world innovate, develop, design, and engineer new ideas—as well as license and sell branding, design, and creativity in the arts. This web application uses the World Bank API  to compare IP against other economic indicators, providing insight on how IP contributes to overall growth.

DataPanino provides data visualizations for hungry analysts. A tool for insight into IP sales in context of other economic indicators. I want to provide a simple tool that helps you see relevant data in the context of overall economic growth. DataPanino allows you to see four major economic indicators together:

    IP, Intellectual Property Sales
    GNI,  Gross National Income
    GDP,  Gross Domestic Product
    FDI,  Foreign Direct Investment

Why choose these seven European countries?

From 1975-2016, the European Union (EU) saw an increase from $1.4B USD to $124B USD in IP sales. That's an 8757% increase. While the United States and China show higher IP sales, the European Union has become highly competitive, especially since 2005. Eurostat states: "Based on the number of trademark applications made in 1996-2015, the leading European countries in terms of EUTM protection are Germany, the United Kingdom, Italy, Spain, France, the Netherlands and Switzerland."

For IP, or "community designs filed," from 2006 to 2016, the same 7 countries hold the highest average in the European Union, with one exception. (For that same period, Poland averaged 2611 community designs filed whereas Switzerland averaged 2227 and the Netherlands averaged 2193.) However, I chose these seven countries based on two criteria: European Union trade mark and IP sales (EUTM and IP). Therefore, I selected Germany, the United Kingdom, Italy, Spain, France, the Netherlands and Switzerland because they ranked highest in both criteria combined.
